# The Collector's Cellar

## Editorial Style Guide

### Version 0.1

---

# Mission
*The Collector's Cellar* exists to become the most comprehensive, historically grounded, and practically useful reference on fine wine collecting ever produced.

It is written for:

- Sommeliers
- Collectors
- Auction specialists
- Merchants
- Importers
- Wine directors
- Students of wine
- Investors
- Curious enthusiasts
The goal is not simply to teach wine.

The goal is to teach how the world's greatest wines become objects of history, luxury, and culture.

---

# Editorial Philosophy
Every statement should answer at least one question:

- What happened?
- Why did it happen?
- Why does it matter?
Facts without context are incomplete.

Stories without evidence are incomplete.

The ideal chapter combines both.

---

# Writing Style
The prose should be:

- Clear
- Elegant
- Historically grounded
- Accessible without being simplistic
- Technically precise
- Engaging without becoming conversational
Avoid unnecessary jargon.

Introduce specialized vocabulary carefully.

Assume intelligence, not prior knowledge.

---

# Every Chapter Includes

1. Learning Objectives
2. Historical Context
3. Core Concepts
4. Technical Discussion
5. Auction Perspective
6. Collector Perspective
7. Sommelier Perspective
8. Case Study
9. Common Misconceptions
10. Key Takeaways
11. Glossary Additions
12. Further Reading

---

# Sources
Whenever possible, prioritize:

- Producer archives
- Château publications
- Auction catalogs
- Historical records
- Academic works
- Books by recognized authorities
Secondary summaries should support, not replace, primary sources.

---

# Tone
Never sensationalize.

Never exaggerate.

If uncertainty exists, acknowledge it.

Distinguish clearly between evidence, interpretation, and opinion.

---

# Long-Term Goal
Create a reference that remains valuable regardless of changes in wine trends.

The emphasis is on enduring principles rather than short-lived fashions.
