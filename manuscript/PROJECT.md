# Project Charter

## The Collector's Cellar

---

## Purpose
To create the definitive modern reference on fine wine, collecting, provenance, auctions, and luxury wine culture.

The work should be rigorous enough to earn the respect of experienced sommeliers, collectors, auction specialists, educators, and serious enthusiasts.

---

## Scope
The project will combine:

- History
- Viticulture
- Economics
- Auction Markets
- Collector Psychology
- Producer Profiles
- Vintage Studies
- Wine Service
- Luxury Business
- Authentication
- Provenance
- Market Analysis

---

## Success Criteria
A successful chapter should:

- Teach something new.
- Explain why it matters.
- Be historically accurate.
- Connect ideas across disciplines.
- Improve the reader's ability to think critically about wine.

---

## Editorial Principles
Depth is preferred over speed.

Accuracy is preferred over certainty.

Evidence is preferred over opinion.

Curiosity is preferred over convention.

Every revision should improve the work.

---

## Long-Term Vision
The Collector's Cellar is intended to remain a living reference.

Future editions should expand, revise, and improve earlier material as scholarship and the wine market evolve.

Version numbers should reflect meaningful editorial progress rather than arbitrary milestones.
