# Canon

## The Collector's Cellar

---

### First Principle
Wine is never merely fermented grape juice.

Every important bottle exists at the intersection of:

- Agriculture
- Geography
- History
- Economics
- Luxury
- Culture
- Human psychology
Whenever possible, chapters should explore all of these dimensions.

---

### Second Principle
Collectors rarely buy wine for only one reason.

A purchase may simultaneously satisfy:

- curiosity
- investment
- nostalgia
- prestige
- generosity
- legacy
The project should acknowledge this complexity.

---

### Third Principle
Auction prices are historical artifacts.

Every record price tells a story about the people, the moment, and the market that produced it.

---

### Fourth Principle
The goal is understanding.

Not memorization.

Facts are useful only when they explain larger patterns.

---

### Fifth Principle
Every chapter should leave the reader seeing wine differently than before they began reading.

If perspective has not changed, the chapter is incomplete.
